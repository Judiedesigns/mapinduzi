About us.

We're a small, deliberately built firm with a single purpose: to shift the development sector from donor-driven dependency toward African-led sustainability. Here's the story of why we exist and how we work.

Find out our work →

"To drive systemic transformation and realize a future of equitable, locally-led, and sustainable development across the continent."

The Mapinduzi Mission

The aid system was built for donors, not communities.

For decades, the development sector has been designed around donor priorities — not community needs. Programs are built to satisfy reporting requirements, not to transfer power. When the funding cycle ends, so does the progress. That's the system we're here to change.

To drive systemic transformation and realize a future of equitable, locally-led, sustainable development across the continent.

Every system we build is created to function without us.
Our conviction

Shifting power back to where it belongs.

Our Mission: to drive lasting transformation, realizing a future where development across Africa is equitable, locally-led, and no longer dependent on outside intervention to survive.

Our Vision: an Africa where external support plays a supporting role — not the leading one. Where local institutions are trusted, capable, and fully in charge of their own progress.

HOW WE WORK

Not just consultants. We are Catalysts for change.

01 Standards

Every program we design starts with listening. We map each community from the bottom up — the culture, the politics, the problem, the people. No assumptions, no imported models, no shortcuts.

02 Delivery

Communities lead. We support. We use co-creation methodologies and planning systems where the people closest to the problem define the solutions — and stay in the driver's seat throughout.

03 Legacy

We plan for the end from the beginning. Every piece of work includes a handover design so that local institutions are stronger, more self-sufficient, and ready to shape what comes next.

The people behind the revolution.

Both of our Founding Partners are African development sector veterans. Together, they have worked in the field and are rooted in finance literacy.

Akinyi Oongo Founding Partner

Akinyi Oongo is a Founding Partner at Mapinduzi, a Pan-African advisory practice with over a decade dedicated to advancing power in philanthropy across Africa. Her work focuses on supporting donors and philanthropy-minded organizations in building stronger, more equitable systems for resource allocation and impactful giving.

She brings extensive experience at the intersection of global health, gender equality, and international funding. Akinyi has managed multimillion-dollar grants across global self-reliance funds and strategic initiatives — coaching organizations to strengthen governance, mobilize resources, and build institutional capacity. She works directly with governments, multilateral agencies, and organizations of all typologies, with a singular focus: ensuring programs are set up not just to deliver, but to last.

Rooted in feminist principles of equity, justice, and collective agency, Akinyi advances funding approaches that are flexible, trust-based, and grounded in local expertise. She works as a thought partner to leaders advancing women's rights, SRHR, and social equity — ensuring they are meaningfully resourced and centered in decision-making.

How we work.

African Excellence

Every program we design starts from an African frame — not as the starting point for development practice, but as the standard it aspires to. African excellence is our measure, our method, and our benchmark.

Evidence-Driven Insights

We build programs that start with knowing, not guessing. Rigorous, participatory research isn't a box to tick — it's the foundation everything else is built on. Local expertise informs everything we do.

Feminist Practice

Equity isn't optional. We centre the voices, rights, and lived realities of the most marginalized — especially women and girls. We don't just talk about systemic change; we design it in from the start.
